Departments

We have various departments to cater to your needs.

Cardiology is the branch of medicine that deals with the study, diagnosis, and treatment of heart and blood vessel disorders. It focuses on conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart attack, heart failure, arrhythmias, hypertension, congenital heart defects, and valvular diseases. Cardiologists use various diagnostic tools like ECG, echocardiography, angiography, stress tests, and cardiac imaging to assess heart health. Treatments include lifestyle changes, medications (such as blood thinners, beta-blockers, and statins), interventional procedures like angioplasty and stent placement, and surgeries including bypass surgery and valve replacement. Preventive cardiology emphasizes regular exercise, a healthy diet, quitting smoking, managing stress, and routine health checkups to lower the risk of cardiovascular diseases, which remain among the leading causes of death worldwide.

Neurology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the study, diagnosis, and treatment of disorders affecting the nervous system, which includes the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves. Neurologists deal with a wide range of conditions such as stroke, epilepsy, migraine, multiple sclerosis, Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, neuropathies, and movement disorders. Diagnostic methods include neurological examinations, MRI, CT scans, EEG, EMG, and lumbar puncture to identify abnormalities in the nervous system. Treatment may involve medications, physical therapy, lifestyle modifications, or surgical interventions in collaboration with neurosurgeons. Neurology also emphasizes preventive care by managing risk factors like hypertension, diabetes, and head injuries, as these can significantly impact brain and nerve health.

Hepatology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the study, diagnosis, and management of diseases related to the liver, gallbladder, biliary tree, and pancreas. It covers a wide range of conditions such as hepatitis (viral, alcoholic, or autoimmune), fatty liver disease, cirrhosis, liver cancer, gallstones, and pancreatic disorders. Since the liver plays a vital role in metabolism, detoxification, and digestion, hepatology is closely linked with gastroenterology but has developed as a specialized field due to the complexity of liver-related diseases. Diagnostic tools include liver function tests, imaging techniques like ultrasound, CT, MRI, and liver biopsy. Treatment approaches vary from lifestyle modifications and medications to advanced procedures like endoscopy, surgery, or liver transplantation in severe cases. Preventive measures in hepatology often focus on vaccination (e.g., hepatitis A and B), avoiding alcohol abuse, and managing metabolic disorders to maintain liver health.

Pediatrics is the branch of medicine that deals with the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents. Pediatricians are trained to diagnose and treat a wide range of conditions, from minor illnesses to serious diseases. They also monitor growth and development, provide preventive care through vaccinations, and offer guidance on nutrition and safety. Pediatric care often involves working closely with families to address the physical, emotional, and social aspects of a child's health.

Orthopedics is the branch of medicine concerned with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of musculoskeletal disorders. Orthopedic surgeons manage a wide range of conditions affecting bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and muscles. Common issues include fractures, arthritis, sports injuries, and congenital deformities. Diagnostic tools such as X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans help identify problems, while treatment options may involve physical therapy, medications, or surgical interventions like joint replacement or arthroscopy. Preventive measures include maintaining a healthy lifestyle, regular exercise, and proper ergonomics to reduce the risk of injury and promote overall musculoskeletal health.
